# Miruwa — pricing & revenue model

> For buyer/investor conversations. Numbers are planning ballparks (HK B2B
> healthcare, early-stage); validate against what the first pilot proves.

## How Miruwa is charged (B2B, per organization — never per clinic-seat)
1. **Per monitored patient / month (PMPM)** — the remote-monitoring standard, scales
   with their panel. Realistic: **HK$50–150 / patient / month** (~US$6–19).
2. **Per-site annual license**, tiered by patient volume (e.g. up to 100 / 500 / 2,000).
3. **+ one-time setup / onboarding fee** per site.
4. **Pilot:** free or nominal (proof, not revenue) → convert to paid after.

Recommended early structure: **free 12-week pilot → then PMPM** (simple to reason
about, scales with their success), with an annual-license option for larger orgs
that prefer a fixed budget line.

## Why there's pricing power
- One avoided readmission saves a hospital **tens of thousands of HK$**. Prevent a
  fraction and the ROI dwarfs a HK$50–150/patient fee.
- The government **CDCC scheme + DHCs** mean there is an existing **budget line** for
  nurse-led chronic-care follow-up — you're funded-adjacent, not net-new spend.

## Realistic revenue ramp (honest)
Healthcare sales cycles run **3–12 months**. Plan for slow, then compounding.

| Stage | Reality | Approx ARR |
|---|---|---|
| Months 0–6 | Free pilots, proving ≥70% response + outcomes | ~$0 |
| First paid site | One program, a few hundred patients | **HK$60k–300k/yr** (~US$8k–38k) |
| 3–5 HK sites | A real solo-founder business | **HK$300k–1.2M/yr** (~US$40k–150k) |
| Scale (many sites + global) | Needs team + funding + multi-market compliance | venture territory |

Concrete math: **1,000 monitored patients × HK$80/mo ≈ HK$960k/yr (~US$123k)**;
5,000 patients ≈ HK$4.8M/yr.

## Pilot → paid conversion
1. Free 12-week pilot, ~20–50 patients, measured on **≥70% check-in response rate**.
2. Deliver the outcome data + board PDF + a testimonial.
3. Convert that site to PMPM (or annual license) and use the case study to sell the
   next 2–3 HK sites.
4. Incorporate (Bosnian obrt to start; US LLC / Estonia for global + Stripe) **when
   money first changes hands** — not before.

*(Not financial advice — ballparks to plan with.)*
